# Comparable Apps

## Purpose

This document reviews existing wildlife and citizen science applications to identify useful features, strengths, weaknesses, and opportunities for Kelowna Wildlife Tracker.

---

# eBird

## Overview

eBird is a bird observation platform managed by the Cornell Lab of Ornithology.

Users can:

* Record bird sightings
* Submit complete checklists
* Track birding activity
* View bird hotspots
* Contribute to scientific research

## Strengths

* Extremely large user base
* High-quality bird data
* GPS-enabled observations
* Strong scientific credibility
* Mobile-friendly

## Weaknesses

* Bird-only focus
* Can feel overwhelming for casual users
* Requires knowledge of bird species

## Features Worth Considering

* GPS location capture
* Interactive maps
* Observation history
* Scientific data standards

---

# iNaturalist

## Overview

iNaturalist allows users to record observations of plants, animals, fungi, and insects.

Observations can include:

* Photos
* GPS coordinates
* Notes
* Species identification

## Strengths

* Supports many species types
* Large community
* AI-assisted identification
* Strong citizen science participation

## Weaknesses

* Can be complex for casual users
* Species identification can be intimidating for beginners

## Features Worth Considering

* Photo-based observations
* Community validation
* Species suggestions
* Observation maps

---

# Merlin Bird ID

## Overview

Merlin Bird ID helps users identify birds using photos, sounds, and location.

## Strengths

* Easy species identification
* Beginner friendly
* Excellent user experience

## Weaknesses

* Bird-only focus
* Not designed for formal wildlife reporting

## Features Worth Considering

* Simple species selection
* Beginner-friendly workflow
* Location-aware species suggestions

---

# Opportunity for Kelowna Wildlife Tracker

Kelowna Wildlife Tracker should focus on simplicity.

Unlike eBird and iNaturalist, the primary goal is not building a social network or species identification platform.

The goal is:

* Quick wildlife reporting
* Accurate GPS capture
* Easy photo upload
* Alignment with BC wildlife submission requirements

The application should reduce friction between seeing wildlife and reporting wildlife.
